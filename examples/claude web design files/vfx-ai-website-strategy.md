## ğŸ“ WRITING STYLE GUIDE

### Voice & Tone

**Authoritative but Accessible**
- Like an industry veteran explaining to a smart colleague
- Not academic stuffiness
- Not breathless tech hype
- Confident in expertise, humble about limitations

**Example of Good Tone:**
> "After testing five AI rotoscoping tools on 100 production shots, one thing became clear: the demos lie. Not intentionallyâ€”but there's a massive gap between controlled test footage and the chaos of real production plates with motion blur, bad lighting, and hair that seems designed to break algorithms."

**Example of Bad Tone:**
> "Our revolutionary methodology leverages cutting-edge deep learning to disrupt traditional VFX workflows..."

### Writing Principles

1. **Lead with the problem, not the solution**
   - Bad: "We built an ML model..."
   - Good: "VFX supervisors waste 6 hours per episode on estimation..."

2. **Use concrete numbers**
   - Bad: "Significantly reduced costs"
   - Good: "Reduced estimation time from 6 hours to 45 minutes"

3. **Show, don't just tell**
   - Include before/after examples
   - Embed visualizations
   - Link to working demos

4. **Acknowledge limitations**
   - Every piece should include what didn't work
   - Discuss edge cases
   - Be honest about generalizability

5. **Bridge technical and strategic**
   - Explain how the technical choice affects business outcome
   - Connect ML metrics to production KPIs
